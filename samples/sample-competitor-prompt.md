# Sample competitor-research prompt

> Used by `/guide` module 6 to teach `/competitive-research`. Overwrite or delete once you have a real research topic.

---

**Research topic**: "How do competitors handle first-time user onboarding for B2B SaaS dashboard products?"

**Context**: We're considering redesigning our onboarding flow (see `samples/sample-epic-idea.md`). Before committing to a design direction, we want to understand what leading and challenger products in the space are doing.

**Scope**:
- Direct competitors: 2 named products in the same pricing tier and feature surface as us.
- Adjacent / aspirational: 1-2 products in related spaces known for excellent onboarding (e.g., Linear, Vercel, Notion, Figma - pick what's genuinely relevant to the dashboard-product category, not just hyped brands).
- Exclude: tools clearly out of our customer's consideration set.

**Questions I want answered**:
1. What's the activation event that each product defines as "successful first use"?
2. How many steps from signup to first value?
3. Does the product use a tour / empty states / sample data / a combination?
4. What's the opt-out path for users who don't want hand-holding?
5. Any shared UX patterns across all of them, or are they all different?

**Deliverable**: A matrix (rows = products, columns = the 5 questions above) with sourced observations. Screenshots or links welcome. Called out clearly: what's an observation vs. what's an inference.

**Non-goals**:
- Pricing analysis (we did that last quarter).
- Feature-by-feature comparison outside onboarding.

**Timeline**: Needed for next week's design review.
