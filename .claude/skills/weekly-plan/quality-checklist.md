# P-Task Quality Checklist

Reference for the validate task quality step in the weekly-plan skill.

## 8 Quality Rules

| # | Rule | Good Example | Bad Example |
|---|------|--------------|-------------|
| **1** | Pick 3-5 **meaty tasks**, not many small ones | "Draft Onboarding Plan for new PM" | "Send reminder email to X" |
| **2** | Completing a task marks a **clear result** | "Create first draft of Strategy doc" | "Work on Strategy doc" |
| **3** | Task is **noteworthy**, not routine | "Define Q2 OKR with team" | "Go through emails since vacation" |
| **4** | Task needs **active effort**, not automatic | "Prepare and conduct meeting with X" | "Conduct scheduled meeting with X" |
| **5** | **Split grouped tasks** into independently achievable items | P1: "Create Whitepaper" / P2: "Prepare Webinar" | "Create Whitepaper and prepare Webinar" |
| **6** | **Highlight priorities**, not full activity list | 3-5 focused tasks | 10 tasks covering everything you'll do |
| **7** | P-Tasks should **help progress strategic goals** | Tasks tied to initiatives/OKRs | Random operational tasks only |
| **8** | On review: task is **completed or not** | "Not completed - didn't find time" | "In progress" |

## Red Flags to Fix

- Vague verbs: "work on", "think about", "look into", "support"
- Too small: "send email", "schedule meeting" (unless part of larger task)
- Automatic: "attend scheduled meeting" (unless significant prep required)
- Grouped: "create X and Y" (should be separate P-tasks)
- No clear completion: "improve X" (improve how? by when?)

## Key Principle

**P-Tasks list what you intend to FOCUS on to get DONE**, not an overview of what keeps you busy.

- Use P-Tasks for: Strategic work, deliverables, decision-making, meaty tasks
- Don't use P-Tasks for: Routine operations, scheduled meetings (unless heavy prep), small emails

**Other activities can go in "Notes" section** (meetings that consume time, time off, context).

## Customization

After a few weeks of use, add your own notes here based on your patterns:
- What types of tasks do you tend to overcommit to?
- What tasks are actually routine and shouldn't be P-tasks?
- What's your realistic weekly capacity?
