# Dana - Backend Developer

An example persona. Replace with your own or use as a structural reference.

## Background

Dana has been a backend developer for seven years, most recently at a mid-size e-commerce company. She works on the payments platform team and has deep experience with distributed systems, API design, and PostgreSQL. She joined her current company from a fintech startup where she was one of three engineers building payment rails from scratch.

She reads technical books, follows half a dozen engineering blogs, and spends weekends contributing to an open-source project she maintains. She is not interested in becoming a manager.

---

## Job role

- **Title**: Senior Backend Engineer
- **Seniority**: Senior (7+ years)
- **Organization type**: Mid-market (~500 engineers)
- **Primary focus**: Payments platform APIs and reliability

---

## Responsibilities

- Design and implement payment processing APIs.
- Review pull requests from team members (3-4 PRs per day).
- Own on-call rotation for payment services (one week every six weeks).
- Mentor two mid-level engineers on the team.
- Participate in architecture reviews for cross-team changes.

---

## Ways of working

- **Tools she lives in**: IDE, terminal, Slack, GitHub, PagerDuty, Datadog.
- **Preferred learning channel**: Official docs first, then source code, then forum / Stack Overflow. Rarely watches videos.
- **Collaboration pattern**: Async-first. Prefers written specs over meetings. Blocks out 4-hour focus windows most mornings.
- **Pace and rhythm**: Two-week sprints. Open to pulling in urgent work but flags it explicitly.

---

## Goals

- **This quarter**: Ship the new refunds API with 99.99% availability.
- **This year**: Reduce on-call page volume for the payments team by 40%.

---

## Pain points

- "The APIs I'm integrating against keep changing without notice. I've burned a full day debugging a silent schema change twice this quarter."
- "Docs are sometimes right, sometimes out of date. I've stopped trusting them as the source of truth and just read the code."
- "Half my PRs get delayed because I can't get a reviewer to pick them up."

---

## Influences

- Senior engineers on her team (strong trust).
- A small set of technical blogs (Charity Majors, Will Larson).
- Open-source communities where she participates.
- Not vendor marketing. She ignores most of it.

---

## Tools

- **Most relevant**: GitHub, Postman, Datadog, her IDE.
- **Adjacent**: Jira (reluctant), Confluence (rarely), Slack (constantly).

---

## Constraints

- Must get security review for any change touching payment data.
- Cannot install arbitrary local tooling; IT-approved list only.
- Production deploys only via the internal CI/CD pipeline.

---

- How much does she care about the quality of non-technical docs (marketing pages, FAQs)?
- What would make her advocate internally for adopting a new tool?
