# Marcus - Operations Manager

An example persona. Replace with your own or use as a structural reference.

## Background

Marcus has run operations teams for twelve years across logistics, retail, and most recently a mid-size SaaS company. He is not technical. He leads a team of ten operations analysts who monitor dashboards, handle escalations, and coordinate with customer support and engineering when things go wrong.

He took one Python course two years ago and can read basic SQL, but writes neither regularly. He uses Excel pivot tables every day and considers them underrated.

---

## Job role

- **Title**: Operations Manager
- **Seniority**: Senior individual contributor / team lead
- **Organization type**: Mid-market SaaS
- **Primary focus**: Incident response coordination and post-mortem ownership

---

## Responsibilities

- Maintain the on-call rotation and escalation paths.
- Own the quarterly incident review with leadership.
- Triage customer complaints that reach operations.
- Report operational metrics weekly to the COO.
- Train new operations analysts (two or three per quarter).

---

## Ways of working

- **Tools he lives in**: Slack, the incident-management tool, email, Excel, and two internal dashboards.
- **Preferred learning channel**: A colleague walking him through it live, then a written cheatsheet he can refer to.
- **Collaboration pattern**: Lots of cross-functional meetings. Prefers quick calls over long email threads.
- **Pace and rhythm**: Interrupt-driven during the day, deep work happens after 5 PM.

---

## Goals

- **This quarter**: Cut the mean time to acknowledge incidents from 8 minutes to under 3.
- **This year**: Move 30% of routine operations work to self-service for customer support, freeing his analysts for harder incidents.

---

## Pain points

- "I have five dashboards. None of them tell me the same story. When the CEO asks 'are we okay right now' I have to check all five."
- "My analysts spend hours a week on tickets that should never reach us. The triage rules are buried in a wiki nobody reads."
- "I don't need another feature; I need the current tools to stop surprising me."

---

## Influences

- His CFO (who signs off on tool budget).
- A peer ops manager group he's in on Slack.
- Industry conferences (once a year).
- Occasional LinkedIn posts from ops thought leaders.

---

## Tools

- **Most relevant**: Incident management tool, dashboarding tool, email.
- **Adjacent**: Slack, the internal wiki, one BI tool he uses quarterly.

---

## Constraints

- Any new tool must integrate with existing SSO.
- Budget approval requires a 6-month ROI case above $5k/month.
- Cannot ask engineering to build operational tooling without a strong business case.

---

## Open questions

- How much does he actually rely on the BI tool versus just asking the data team?
- Would he pay for a tool that consolidated the five dashboards, or is "people like what they're used to" stronger?
